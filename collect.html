A road to gaming in the old school

Collect

Starting a collection is a little like throwing a grenade: once the pin is pulled and the spoon flips, you got to get things moving in a direction so it doesn't all blow up in your face. Here are just a few things to keep in mind as you get started.

Pick your Potion

For most of us, there is no cheat code to give infinite money. Certainly none for infinite space, either, or max patience on the part of your spouce or significant other. So if you're thinking of getting into collecting retro gaming related items, it's best if you start by finding your passion. Instead of collecting every NES title, maybe do all your favorites from childhood, or the top 50 online. Pick a system, or a genre, or an console generation that appeals to you. Or you could start with instruction manuals. I wouldn't judge.

For me, my personal focus is controllers. To me, few things speak to the artistry, identity, and feel of a gaming system than how you inter act with it . Here is a gallery of some of my favorites.

Where to Look

There's a lot of people on the internet. Most of them will gladly bilk you of your shillings given half a chance. So as a rule of thumb, the closer they are to you, the less likely they want to reach quite as deeply into you pocket. Below is a list of the best sources for gaining those coveted no-longer-in-production gems from yesteryear. All of these are viable sources, and all of them can turn on you like your brother in a foam noodle fight, but the ones at the top of the list are more likely to treat you fairly.

  1. People you know
  2. Garage sales
  3. Online community pages
  4. Thrift stores
  5. Auction sites

And when in doubt, verify. With price tracking sites like Pricecharting.com or GameValueNow.com, or many others Google can guide you to, you can avoid your slick uncle Charles pulling a fast one on you with his one-of-a-kind, vintage, first-edition loose Duck Hunt cartrage.

Tracking

Once your precious collection starts rolling, in addition to how you display it, you might want to start thinking of how you are going to keep track of it. The main difference between a collection and trash heap is organization, and you really don't want to be the next subject of a Horders episode, and bring shame on your family name for generations.

And believe me: it can get rolling in a hurry. So best find a way to sort 'em soon.

Spreadsheets
Welcome to the wild world of modern information tracking. Weather you are a wily veteran of Excel or cruise on the free wings of Google Sheets, you've probably at least dealt with spreadsheeting before. While not always flashy, a little savvy here can help you keep your stuff organized your way.
Apps
You always have your phone, and if you don't, you're a shame and an outcast in modern society.
The good thing about always packing a portable phone of the smart variety are the apps that make your life easier, and there are plenty to be had that want to help you keep your loot organized.
