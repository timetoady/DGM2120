Standard troubleshooting steps

For when your game console doesn't work

Problem Checks Common fix
Console won't power on Plugged in, both sides? Working AC adapter? Consoles are hardy, but some do eventually die. More often the AC adapter needs replacement, but if you have to, try checking on the power coupling inside the shell for wear.
No image AV cables in place? Cables intact? TV on the right input? Various possiblities, but checking AV cables (cheap ones are readily available) is a first step before you go mucking about with the internal connections.
Ugly, smeary graphics AV cables firmly inserted? Contacts clean? TV properly set? When all else is right with a gaming setup, the TV is often wrong. Changing settings to accept older (especially 240p) sources helps, but upscalers and line doublers help more! Most recommended right now is the OSSC, for best ballance of availability, price, and quality.
Flashing screen Pressed power several times? AV firmly inserted? Correct cartrige/CD type? The original NES was especially prone to this. Cleaning helps, but sometimes the contact pins need to be re-set or replaced. Both are methods are available widely online, but I recommend saving your old pins if you can. Some of the replacement ones are too tight, and can wear down your game's contacts.
Hard to get games to work Cartriges/CD completely in? No obvious cracks or gouges? Varies by system, but cartriges and consoles need to be clean! DO NOT BLOW IN THEM! Use air dusters, a cotton swab, and high % rubbing alcohol on contact surfaces for cartriges, and plastic cleaner for discs. For a CD system, some lasers were known to get out of alignment (PS1), so that's worth checking out.
