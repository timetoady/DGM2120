Collect. Preserve. Display. Play.

Here you are...

You see yourself staring down a familiar sight. The trappings, boxes, sounds, and sights of days gone by glitter around you like gemstones in a river.

Digging in a box one day, you find a forgotten copy of Tetris for GameBoy, dusty but carefully preserved. You wipe at it with your fingers, and give it a sharp dusting with a breath. Looking at the faded picture of those shapes falling from a vanishing point above brings back a flood of memories.

Nights crouched near a lamp or with a flashlight in your teeth. Road trips with your face pressed against the window, trying to get that perfect sunbeam angle. Summers of your mom or dad telling you to put it down and go outside, but you're positive you can beat your high score this time...

Now you start pawing around in the box some more, wondering what other treasure might be inside. Maybe the GameBoy itself is somewhere in there. Would it even still work? Maybe you could fix it? Maybe you can find another...

Maybe that's how it began for you. And before you know it, you're a retro gamer. A collector.

“The whole point of collecting is the thrill of acquisition, which must be maximized, and maintained at all costs.”
― John Baxter, A Pound of Paper: Confessions of a Book Addict

While the quote above may be true to most collecting, collecting retro gaming goods holds far wider range in its satisfactions. Undoubted, there is still that "thrill of acquisition" in finding that one copy of Mega Man 6 to complete your collection in a bargin bin at a thrift store. But the difference between collecting games and other passtimes involving the accumlation of stuff is once you obtain it, you can interact with it.

Yes, you can put it in plastic or glass or boxes and store it, but some of the real joy of gaming is pulling out an original copy of Chrono Trigger, powering on a CRT with its iconic debossing twang, firing it up a SNES, and reliving the golden days of youth in 1996. Or 1986. Or 1976.

Or maybe play it in a whole new way you never knew about.

Choose your own adventure

Here, you'll find a humble guide on your retro gaming journey, complete with your own branching paths and detours and hints when you get stuck. There are as many ways to take it as there are people, but in general, here's a few broad paths you can try:

  • Collect: the game of aquisition
  • Preserve: the care of aged electronics, plastiics, and paper
  • Display: setting up your collection in just the right way for your circumstances
  • Play: retro gaming is an interactive medium

Despite the passage of decades and the increase in scarcity, there are actually more ways to interact with games than ever before!
